Going to the U.S

Have you ever stood alone at a large party? Have you ever wondered what special features you have for people to notice you? Have you ever felt disoriented as you gradually became separated from a society, when time is always passing without waiting for the answer to the question "What are you gonna do?" from you? Those are the things I've been through before. I immigrated from Vietnam to the US the year after I finished grade 11 with a future, a passion that is not clear.

 

Communication is also a problem for me as the cultures in America are very different from where I used to live. My days to school are tedious when all I do in a day is just going to school by bus, studying, getting on the bus to go home, and doing my homework. I made them all alone in my senior year of high school.

“I think the memories I have in Vietnam are precious things. People will change; time will obscure our emotions and bring us back to reality.”

— 2018—
